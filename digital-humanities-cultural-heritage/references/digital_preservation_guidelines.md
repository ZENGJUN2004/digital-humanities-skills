# Digital Preservation Guidelines for Cultural Heritage

## Overview
This document provides comprehensive guidelines for the digital preservation of cultural heritage materials. It covers best practices, standards, and strategies for ensuring the long-term access to and sustainability of digital cultural heritage resources.

## Fundamentals of Digital Preservation

### 1. Definition and Scope
- **Digital Preservation**: The process of managing digital materials over time to ensure their accessibility, authenticity, and integrity
- **Scope**: Includes all types of digital cultural heritage materials, such as digital surrogates of physical artifacts, born-digital cultural content, and digital humanities project outputs

### 2. Key Concepts
- **Authenticity**: The assurance that a digital object is what it purports to be
- **Integrity**: The assurance that a digital object has not been altered in an unauthorized manner
- **Accessibility**: The ability to locate and retrieve digital objects
- **Sustainability**: The capacity to maintain digital preservation activities over the long term
- **Interoperability**: The ability of systems and software to exchange and use information

### 3. Preservation Challenges
- **Technological Obsolescence**: Hardware, software, and format obsolescence
- **Media Degradation**: Physical degradation of storage media
- **Content Drift**: Changes in content over time
- **Organizational Sustainability**: Institutional and financial challenges
- **Legal and Ethical Issues**: Copyright, privacy, and cultural sensitivity

## Digital Preservation Strategies

### 1. Format Management
- **Format Assessment**: Evaluating the risk level of digital formats
- **Format Migration**: Converting digital objects to more sustainable formats
- **Emulation**: Using software to simulate outdated hardware or software environments
- **Normalization**: Converting digital objects to standard formats

### 2. Metadata Management
- **Preservation Metadata**: Information needed to preserve and manage digital objects
- **Descriptive Metadata**: Information needed to discover and understand digital objects
- **Technical Metadata**: Information about the technical characteristics of digital objects
- **Administrative Metadata**: Information about the management of digital objects

### 3. Storage and Bit Preservation
- **Redundant Storage**: Multiple copies in different locations
- **Storage Media**: Selection and management of appropriate storage media
- **Bit-Level Preservation**: Ensuring the integrity of digital bits
- **Checksum Verification**: Regular verification of digital object integrity

### 4. Access Management
- **Access Strategies**: Balancing preservation and access needs
- **Access Systems**: Systems for providing access to preserved digital objects
- **Rights Management**: Managing intellectual property rights
- **Privacy Protection**: Protecting sensitive information

### 5. Preservation Planning
- **Risk Assessment**: Identifying and evaluating preservation risks
- **Preservation Goals**: Establishing clear preservation objectives
- **Action Planning**: Developing detailed preservation action plans
- **Monitoring and Evaluation**: Regular assessment of preservation activities

## Standards and Best Practices

### 1. Metadata Standards
- **Dublin Core**: Basic descriptive metadata standard
- **MODS (Metadata Object Description Schema)**: Library-focused metadata standard
- **EAD (Encoded Archival Description)**: Standard for archival finding aids
- **PREMIS (Preservation Metadata Implementation Strategies)**: Standard for preservation metadata
- **DCML (Dublin Core Metadata Language)**: Extension of Dublin Core

### 2. File Format Standards
- **Text**: PDF/A, plain text (UTF-8)
- **Images**: TIFF, JPEG 2000
- **Audio**: WAV, FLAC
- **Video**: MP4 (H.264), Matroska
- **Data**: CSV, XML, JSON

### 3. Technical Standards
- **OAIS (Open Archival Information System)**: Reference model for digital preservation systems
- **ISO 16363**: Audit and certification of trustworthy digital repositories
- **NDSA Levels of Digital Preservation**: Framework for assessing digital preservation maturity
- **DPN (Digital Preservation Network)**: Community-based preservation network

### 4. Organizational Standards
- **ICA (International Council on Archives)**: Standards for archival practices
- **IFLA (International Federation of Library Associations)**: Standards for library practices
- **CEN (European Committee for Standardization)**: European standards for cultural heritage
- **UNESCO**: International standards for cultural heritage preservation
